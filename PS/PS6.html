The paper was based on a field experiment conducted by an auto insurance company in the southeastern United States. Customers were asked to report the current odometer reading of up to four cars covered by their policy. They were randomly assigned to sign a statement indicating, I promise that the information I am providing is true either at the top or bottom of the form.

Customers assigned to the 'sign-at-the-top' condition reported driving 2,400 more miles than those assigned to the 'sign-at-the-bottom' condition. This was seen as evidence that signing at the top could be a cheap and effective way of reducing dishonesty.
